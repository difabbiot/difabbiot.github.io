Current Project

LINERFUN

A 36-month Marie Skłodowska-Curie Global Fellowship combining high-fidelity scale-resolving simulations, physics-informed impedance boundary conditions, and machine learning to redesign acoustic liners for quieter UHBR jet engines.

Physics-informed Time-Domain Impedance Boundary Conditions for Scale-Resolving Simulations of Acoustic Liners in UHBR Engines

MSCA Global Fellowship Grant 101206371 May 2026 – May 2029 € 309,153.72

Acoustic liners are the primary noise-reduction technology inside turbofan nacelles. As engines evolve towards ultra-high bypass ratios (UHBR), shorter and wider nacelles leave less wall area for liners — making their design critically more demanding. LINERFUN develops physics-informed Time-Domain Impedance Boundary Conditions (piTDIBC) suitable for scale-resolving CFD/CAA, enabling accurate, low-cost optimisation of next-generation liners.
