LINERFUN

Tony Di Fabbio, Dr.-Ing.

MSCA Global Postdoctoral Fellow · Aeroacoustics & Computational Fluid Dynamics

What is LINERFUN? Aircraft engines generate most of their noise through turbulent airflow interacting with surfaces inside the engine nacelle. Acoustic liners — panels of tiny cavities lining the engine walls — absorb that noise. LINERFUN develops new physics-informed simulation methods to predict how well these liners work under realistic flow conditions, with the goal of helping engineers design quieter engines for the next generation of high-bypass turbofans.
