Areas of expertise

My research sits at the intersection of high-fidelity fluid simulation and data-driven modelling, applied to turbulent flows in aerospace engineering. Below are the four main areas where I can contribute to research projects, collaborations, or consultancy engagements.

🤖
Machine learning for turbulence modelling
Standard engineering simulations use simplified turbulence models that often fail in complex flows. I develop methods to make these models smarter by embedding corrections learned from high-fidelity data — using techniques such as gene expression programming and field inversion — without sacrificing computational speed.
Gene Expression Programming Field Inversion Spalart–Allmaras model OpenFOAM EVE3 Python

Scale-resolving simulations of turbulent flows
When engineering accuracy is not enough, high-fidelity methods that resolve the turbulent motion directly are required. I have designed and run large-eddy simulations (LES) and scale-adaptive simulations (SAS) of aerodynamically complex configurations, including on GPU-accelerated supercomputers and national HPC facilities.
Large-Eddy Simulation Scale-Adaptive Simulation HiPSTAR OpenFOAM GPU computing SuperMUC-NG

Aeroacoustics & acoustic liner modelling
Aircraft engine noise is increasingly regulated, and acoustic liners are one of the primary tools to control it. My current MSCA research (LINERFUN) develops physics-informed time-domain impedance boundary conditions (piTDIBC) that allow accurate simulation of liner performance under realistic turbulent flow — a capability missing from current industrial design workflows.
piTDIBC Impedance boundary conditions CAA / CFD coupling HiPSTAR UHBR engines Liner design

Vortex-dominated & high-speed aerodynamics
My doctoral research focused on the flow physics over highly swept delta wings — configurations relevant to combat aircraft and future urban air mobility platforms — where strong leading-edge vortices dominate the aerodynamic behaviour and interact with shocks at transonic speeds. I characterised vortex breakdown mechanisms and contributed to the understanding of how standard CFD tools fail to capture them.
RANS / hybrid RANS-LES Delta wing aerodynamics Vortex breakdown Transonic flow Shock–vortex interaction Wind tunnel validation
