Biography

About Me

I am an aerospace engineer with a strong background in computational fluid dynamics, turbulence modelling, and aeroacoustics. Born in Campobasso, Italy, I completed my BSc and MSc at Politecnico di Torino (both with honours, 110/110 cum laude), with an Erasmus+ exchange at Technische Universität München and a year at École Centrale Paris as part of the POLITO Young Talent programme.

I did my PhD at the Universität der Bundeswehr München (funded by Airbus Defence & Space), graduating Summa cum Laude in 2024 with a thesis on turbulence modelling for vortical flows from swept leading edges. My doctoral work combined high-fidelity scale-resolving simulations, RANS modelling, and data-driven techniques, with extensive HPC use on the LRZ SuperMUC-NG cluster (~60M CPU hours).

After a DAAD-funded visiting period at the University of Melbourne and a postdoctoral fellowship at Leonardo Innovation Labs, I am now starting my MSCA Global Fellowship LINERFUN at Politecnico di Torino, working on acoustic liners for UHBR engines.
